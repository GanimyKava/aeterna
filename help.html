Image-based AR

1
Open the Image-based AR page

Tap on "Image-based AR" from the main menu

2
Allow camera access

When prompted, allow the app to access your device's camera

3
Point at trained images

Point your camera at the trained images (like Uluru or MCG posters/images). Keep the image in view and steady

Marker-based AR

1
Open the Marker-based AR page

Tap on "Marker-based AR" from the main menu

2
Allow camera access

Grant camera permissions when requested

3
Use printed markers

Point your camera at printed AR markers or barcodes. You can use standard AR markers like Hiro marker

4
Experience AR content

When a marker is detected, AR content will appear aligned with the marker. Keep the marker in view to continue the experience

üí° Tip: Print markers on white paper with good contrast for best results. Keep the marker flat and well-lit.

Location-based AR

1
Open the Location-based AR page

Tap on "Location-based AR" from the main menu

2
Allow location access

Grant location permissions so the app can detect when you're near tourist attractions

3
Visit the attraction

Travel to a configured tourist attraction (like Uluru or MCG). The app will automatically detect when you're within range

4
View AR content

When you're within the attraction's radius, historical videos will automatically appear. Point your camera around to explore

üí° Tip: Ensure GPS/location services are enabled and you have a clear view of the sky for accurate location detection.
